How TrendPulse Works

📡 Data Sources

📈
Google Trends — The backbone of every score. We query US interest-over-time for each keyword across the past 12 months. Google normalises interest to a 0–100 scale where 100 = peak popularity for that term, so values are relative, not absolute search volumes. Data is fetched in batches of 5 with rate-limit protection and 12-second pauses between batches.
🔶
Reddit — We query Reddit's public search API for each keyword and count posts from the past 30 days and past 7 days. Week-over-week velocity reveals whether community buzz is accelerating or cooling. No account or API key required — Reddit's public JSON endpoint is used directly with a respectful 1.5s delay between requests.
📦
Amazon — We scrape the first page of Amazon search results for each keyword to extract: total listing count (competition proxy), Best Seller and Amazon's Choice badges, average price across the top 10 products, average star rating, and the highest review count (demand proxy). Scraping uses randomised 2.5–4.5s delays and rotating user-agents.

🧮 Scores & Formulas

📊
12-Month Growth % — Compares the most recent month's Google Trends index against the earliest non-zero month in the 12-month window.
(recent_month − earliest_month) / earliest_month × 100
A keyword at index 10 in January and 50 in December shows +400% growth.
🏆
Trend Score (0–100) — A composite signal weighting current interest heavily (is it high RIGHT NOW?) with momentum (how fast is it growing?).
score = 0.6 × current_interest + 0.4 × min(growth / 50, 100)
⚡
Reddit Velocity — Week-over-week change in post volume.
(this_week_posts − last_week_posts) / last_week_posts × 100
Shows ▲ if mentions are accelerating, ▼ if slowing, → if flat.

🎯 Product Viability Score (0–100)

💡
The Viability Score is designed specifically for entrepreneurs evaluating whether a topic represents a real opportunity for a new product. It combines five factors that research shows predict new product success, weighted by importance:
30
Trend Momentum
Growth % — the sweet spot is Hot (200–999%). Breakout is penalised slightly for volatility risk; Flat is zeroed out.
20
Current Interest
The Google Trends index for the most recent month — is demand live right now, or just historical?
25
Competition
Amazon listing count — fewer listings means easier to rank, stand out, and win market share. <500 = strong; >15K = crowded.
10
Price Viability
Average Amazon price — higher prices leave room for marketing, manufacturing, and profit margin. <$20 is a race to the bottom.
15
Social Demand
Reddit mentions (30d) + velocity bonus — organic community interest is the strongest signal that demand is real and not manufactured.
➕
Bonuses & Penalties — Best Seller or Amazon's Choice badge adds +3 (proves buyers exist). Top product with 1,000+ reviews adds +2 (market validated). Average rating below 3.5★ subtracts −3 (quality gap may be hard to overcome). Flat topics are hard-capped at 30 regardless of other factors.
75–100 · Strong Opportunity 60–74 · Good Potential 45–59 · Moderate Risk 30–44 · High Risk 1–29 · Not Recommended

🏷️ Card Status Labels

🚀 Breakout — 1,000%+ growth 🔥 Hot — 200–999% growth ↗ Rising — steady upward trend ⬛ Flat — peaked, no longer climbing. Card stays forever; data frozen.

🔄 Update Schedule

🌙
The nightly job runs at 1 AM CST (7 AM UTC) via GitHub Actions. It fetches fresh Google Trends, Reddit, and Amazon data for all active keywords, recalculates every score including Viability, runs the discovery engine to find up to 20 new keywords, then commits the updated cache — triggering an automatic Railway redeploy within ~2 minutes. Flat keywords are skipped during fetching to save time, but their last known data and scores remain visible forever.

🔍 Keyword Discovery

🤖
Each night, pytrends' related_queries() API is called on seed terms across all 13 categories. Rising related queries that look like product names (not questions or how-tos) are automatically added to the tracking list — up to 20 per night. The list grows organically toward its practical ceiling of ~400–500 keywords. Every newly discovered keyword gets a full Viability Score on its first night.
